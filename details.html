From Atlanta to Everywhere

September 13, 1996 holds weight in hip-hop history — the day we lost Tupac Shakur, but also the day Jordan Terrell Carter was born in Riverdale, Georgia. The world would come to know him as Playboi Carti, a genre-bending artist who helped rewrite the rules of rap.

Carti began rapping under the name $ir Cartier in 2011, later changing it as his SoundCloud loosies like Broke Boi and Fetti became underground anthems. He built a name in Atlanta’s scene before relocating to New York, linking with A$AP Mob, and signing to Interscope — entering the Cash Carti era.

With a voice that floated between whisper and chant, and a look blending goth, punk, and high fashion, Carti became more than a rapper. His 2017 debut mixtape and 2018’s Die Lit made waves, but his mysterious silence afterward only deepened his cult status. When he returned in 2020 with Whole Lotta Red, he emerged fully reborn as Vamp Carti — chaotic, raw, post-genre.

In 2019, he founded Opium, a label and collective pushing a new wave of experimental artists like Ken Carson and Destroy Lonely. Then, in 2025, he released his third studio album, Music (I Am Music) — proving once again that Carti doesn’t follow trends. He is the trend.

More than music, Carti is energy. A symbol of rebellion, fashion, and youth subculture — forever evolving, never explaining.
